# Skill — Abbreviation Discipline

Abbreviations follow a strict lifecycle: define once at first mention, then use exclusively thereafter. The goal is to reduce cognitive load — the reader learns the abbreviation once and never encounters the full form again.

## When to invoke

- Checking abbreviation consistency during full-text polish.
- Confirming first-use definition when introducing new terminology.

## Convention

At first mention, write the full name followed by the abbreviation in parentheses: `Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)`. After definition, use `CNN` exclusively — do not revert to the full name.

The Abstract and Introduction define abbreviations independently. An abbreviation defined in the Abstract must be re-defined at its first occurrence in the Introduction, because some readers enter the paper at the Introduction.

Only abbreviate terms that appear ≥3 times in the paper. Terms appearing 1–2 times should remain in full form — the abbreviation saves no space and adds cognitive overhead.

## Pitfalls

- Reverting to the full name after defining the abbreviation (wastes space and appears unprofessional).
- Excessive abbreviations increasing cognitive load on the reader.
- Using an abbreviation in the Abstract without defining it (some readers only read the Abstract).
